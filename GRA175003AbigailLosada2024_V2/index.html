Our perfect cut-out cookie recipe brings Santa’s favorite flavors right to your kitchen. The blend of warm spices and molasses gives these cookies the cozy, seasonal taste Santa loves, while the combination of butter and shortening makes the dough easy to roll out and ensures they stay picture-perfect for decorating.

Ingredients

Cookies

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour,plus more for dusting
  • 1 Tablespoon ground ginger
  • 2 Teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 Teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 Teaspoon fine salt
  • ¼ Teaspoon ground all spice
  • ¼ Teaspoon ground cloves
  • 6 Tablespoons unsalted melted butter
  • 1 Teaspoon melted vegtable shortening
  • ⅔ Cup light brown sugar
  • ¾ Cup molases
  • 1 Large Egg

Royal Icing

  • 3 ½ Cups confectioners; sugar
  • 2 Tablespoons meringue powder
  • Food Coloring(optional)

Directions

For The Cookies

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, ginger, cinnamon, baking soda, salt, allspice, and cloves. The warm spices make these cookies Santa-approved!
  2. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, blend the butter, shortening, brown sugar, and molasses until smooth.
  3. Add an egg to give the cookies a touch of holiday cheer. Slowly mix in the dry ingredients, one batch at a time, until the dough comes together.
  4. Divide the dough into two portions, wrap in plastic, and pat each to a ½ inch thickness. Chill in the North Pole’s refrigerator (or yours) for 2 hours.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350°F
  6. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one piece of dough to a ¼inch thickness. Use a gingerbread man cutter to cut the cookies. Dust off any excess flour and refrigerate the cookies on a baking sheet for 15 minutes. Repeat with the second portion of dough.
  7. Place the cookies in bathces on a baking sheet and bake for 12 to 15 minutes, until golden brown around the edges
  8. Transfer to wire rack and let cool completely.

For the Icing

  1. In a mixer, whisk the confectioner’s sugar with the meringue powder. Add 6 tablespoons of water and mix until stiff, glossy peaks form. Add water a tablespoon at a time to achieve the perfect consistency for piping.
  2. Add in food coloring (optional)
  3. Fill pastry bags and let the decorating begin!
